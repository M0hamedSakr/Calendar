Cookie Policy

Last Updated: November 17, 2025

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

2. How We Use Cookies

ChronoWavel uses cookies to:

  • Keep you signed in to your account
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Understand how you use our service
  • Improve our website performance
  • Provide personalized content
  • Analyze usage patterns and trends

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, authentication, and session management.

  • Authentication: Keep you logged in
  • Security: Protect against fraud and abuse
  • Session: Maintain your session state

3.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferences.

  • Theme preference: Remember your dark/light mode choice
  • Language: Remember your language preference
  • Settings: Store your calendar view preferences

3.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

  • Google Analytics: Track page views and usage patterns
  • Performance monitoring: Identify technical issues
  • Feature usage: Understand which features are most popular

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies track your browsing habits to enable us to show advertising which is more likely to be of interest to you.

  • Ad targeting: Show relevant advertisements
  • Conversion tracking: Measure ad campaign effectiveness
  • Retargeting: Show ads on other websites

4. Third-Party Cookies

We use services from third parties who may also set cookies. These include:

  • Google Analytics: Web analytics
  • Google Fonts: Font hosting
  • Stripe: Payment processing
  • Intercom: Customer support chat

These third parties have their own privacy policies and cookie policies.

5. Cookie Duration

Cookies can be either session cookies or persistent cookies:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them

6. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

6.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • See what cookies you have
  • Delete cookies individually or all at once
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites
  • Block all cookies

7. Impact of Blocking Cookies

If you choose to block cookies, some parts of our website may not function properly. Specifically:

  • You may not be able to stay logged in
  • Your preferences won't be saved
  • Some features may not work correctly
  • The website experience may be degraded
